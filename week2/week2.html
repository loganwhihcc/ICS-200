The Band

The Eagles are an American rock band formed in Los Angeles, California in 1971. With multiple number one singles, number one albums, Grammy and Musuc awards, they're one of the most successful bands of all time. They're one of the world's best selling bands, selling more than 200 million records worldwide. They're also one of the most enduring bands, with a career spanning 52 years. They formed in 1971, broke up in 1980, reunited in 1994, quit in 2016 due to Glenn Frey's death, and came back a year to tour again, which is where they've been ever since. Currently, they're on their final tour called "The Long Goodbye Tour" which is still ongoing.

The Members

The band has had multiple lineup changes in their career. Current members are Don Henley(Drums, Guitar, Vocals), Joe Walsh(Guitar, Keyboards, Vocals), Timothy B. Schmit(Bass, Vocals), Deacon Frey(Guitar, Vocals), and Vince Gill(Guitar, Vocals). Former members include Glenn Frey(Guitar, Vocals, Keyboards), Don Felder(Guitar, Banjo, Mandolin, Pedal Steel Guitar, Organ, Vocals), Bernie Leadon(Guitar, Banjo, Mandolin, Pedal Steel Guitar, Vocals), and Randy Meisner(Bass, Vocals). There have also been many touring musicians who have played with them as a part of their backup band.

The Songs

The band had created an incredible catalog of songs, including 5 number one singles. Their greatest hits include but are not limited to "Take It Easy", "Witchy Woman", "Peaceful Easy Feeling", "Desperado", "Tequila Sunrise", "Already Gone", "Best of My Love", "One of These Nights", "Lyin Eyes", "Take It To The Limit", "Hotel California", "New Kid In Town", "Life In The Fast Lane", "The Long Run", "I Can't Tell You Why", "Heartache Tonight", "Get Over It", "Love Will Keep Us Alive", and "How Long". They have also incorporated songs from their solo careers into their shows.
